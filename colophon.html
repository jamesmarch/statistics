About

This ongoing project is intended as a tool for undergraduate in the visual arts to gain a better understanding of the basic principles surrounding statistics and probability testing. It is meant as a supporting component of many first-year statistics textbooks. A few examples include:

  • Crawley, M. J., & Safari Books - York University. (2015). Statistics: An introduction using R (Second edition.). John Wiley & Sons.
  • Hand, D. J. (2008). Statistics: A very short introduction. Oxford; New York: Oxford University Press.
  • Lee, P. M. (2004). Bayesian statistics: An introduction (3rd ed.). London: Arnold.
  • Wagner, S. F. (1992). Introduction to statistics. New York: HarperPerennial.

Author

James March is an interdisciplinary designer and educator who creates all forms of printed and digital matter, including identities, user experiences, front-end interfaces, custom lettering and interactive installations. His education includes a Master of Design from York University, a Master of Science in Experimental Psychology and a Bachelor of Science (Honours) in Psychology from Memorial University. Over the past eight years March has taught interactivity design, typography, statistics and health psychology and has been a speaker and visiting critic at conferences and institutions in Canada, the USA and the United Kingdom. March is currently a full-time professor at Sheridan College in Oakville, Canada.
